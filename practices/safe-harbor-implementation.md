# Safe Harbor Implementation

> **Status:** Placeholder. This page is part of the framework skeleton. Community contributions fill in the substance over time — [open a PR](https://github.com/disclose/dioterms).

Publishing safe harbor language in your VDP is necessary but not sufficient. Making safe harbor *operational* requires aligning Legal, TOS/AUP, platform agreements, and internal procedures so that a researcher who relies on the policy actually gets the protection it promises.

## Planned sections

- **Internal alignment** — the TOS/AUP carve-out, the anti-hacking-law commitment, the anti-circumvention-law commitment
- **What "good faith" means in practice** — how triage communicates this, how to respond when it's disputed
- **Legal team onboarding** — the conversation to have with counsel before launching
- **Handling third-party claims** — what it looks like when a researcher is sued or threatened
- **Regional considerations** — jurisdictions where specific additional language is needed (US CFAA/DMCA, EU NIS2, etc.)
- **Platform interactions** — what hosting providers, CDNs, and marketplaces expect
- **When safe harbor is revoked** — criteria, process, researcher appeal

## How to contribute

Open a PR against `practices/safe-harbor-implementation.md`. Legal reviewers — you are especially invited. Citations to case law and regulatory guidance are welcome.
