# Coordinated Disclosure

> **Status:** Placeholder. This page is part of the framework skeleton. Community contributions fill in the substance over time — [open a PR](https://github.com/disclose/dioterms).

Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (CVD) is the mechanism by which a researcher and a vendor agree on the time, content, and channels of a public disclosure. Get it right and everyone looks good; get it wrong and a vulnerability becomes a headline.

## Planned sections

- **Default timelines** — 90 days, 120 days, "when fixed" — when each is appropriate
- **Timeline negotiation** — valid reasons to extend, invalid reasons, researcher leverage
- **Public disclosure mechanics** — CVE issuance, advisory authoring, researcher credit
- **Multi-party coordination** — when more than one vendor is affected
- **Attack surface considerations** — when a fix is impossible, what alternatives exist
- **Escalation paths** — CERT/CC, national CSIRTs, broker involvement
- **Post-disclosure** — measuring impact, retrospectives, updating the policy
